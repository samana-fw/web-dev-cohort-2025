About Ghazni

Ghazni Province is one of the important, historical, and populous provinces of southeastern Afghanistan, with the city of Ghazni as its capital. Due to its strategic location on the Kabul–Kandahar highway, its diverse ethnic composition (Pashtun, Hazara, Tajik, and others), and its rich cultural heritage, it is also recognized as the “Cultural Capital of the Islamic World.” Ghazni has a significant place in Afghan history, having been the capital of the Ghaznavid Empire in the 10th and 11th centuries. The province is home to many historical sites, including ancient fortresses, mosques, and mausoleums, reflecting its rich cultural heritage. The economy of Ghazni is primarily based on agriculture, livestock, and trade, with many residents engaged in farming and herding.
