About Parwan Province

Parwan Province is one of the central provinces of Afghanistan, located just north of Kabul. It is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and strategic importance as a gateway between the capital and northern regions of the country. The province is home to famous historical sites such as Jabal Saraj Palace and the ancient city of Begram (Kapisa), which played an important role during the Greco-Bactrian period. Parwan also features beautiful landscapes, including mountains, valleys, and rivers that make it a notable destination for nature lovers. Parwan’s economy is mainly based on agriculture, with wheat, grapes, and fruits being the primary products. The province is culturally diverse and known for its hospitality, traditions, and strong community life.

Recreational Areas

Topdara Stupa

Topdara Stupa is an ancient Buddhist stupa dating back around 2000 years, making it one of Parwan’s oldest historical sites. The site provides insight into the religious and cultural history of the region and is highly valued by archaeologists and history enthusiasts.

Jabal Saraj Palace

Jabal Saraj Palace is one of the most beautiful historical landmarks in Parwan Province . Built in the early 20th century, it was originally used to welcome guests and officials. The palace is surrounded by lush gardens and beautifully decorated halls. Today, it is open to visitors and hosts cultural events and festivals.

Bagram Fortress

This area contains the ruins of the ancient city of Bagram, a key site in Central Asian history.
